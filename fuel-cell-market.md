<p><p>The fuel cell market encompasses several types, each with distinct applications and characteristics. Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFCs) are favored for their efficiency in transportation and portable power due to their quick start-up times. Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs) are ideal for stationary power generation, offering high efficiency and fuel flexibility. Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFCs) are used in large-scale applications, while Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFCs) are suitable for commercial settings. Other technologies include alkaline and direct methanol fuel cells, catering to niche markets.</p></p>
