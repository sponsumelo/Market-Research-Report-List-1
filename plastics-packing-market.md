<p><p>The plastics packing market features various material types, each serving specific applications. PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) is widely used for bottles and containers due to its strength and recyclability. HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) is known for its durability and resistance, commonly used in containers and bags. LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) is flexible and often utilized in film products. PP (Polypropylene) offers excellent chemical resistance, making it suitable for food packaging and automotive applications. Other materials include polystyrene and PVC, catering to niche uses.</p></p>
